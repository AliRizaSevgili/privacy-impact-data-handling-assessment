# Methodology Note

## Purpose

This document explains the assessment methodology used for the Privacy Impact & Data Handling Risk Assessment: Customer-Facing Reservation Platform project.

The methodology is designed for a portfolio-based privacy assessment. It is structured, evidence-linked, and Canadian privacy-principles-oriented, but it is not an official regulatory methodology or legal opinion.

## Methodology Overview

The assessment followed nine steps:

1. Define system context and assumptions.
2. Identify personal information elements.
3. Map data flows across workflows and vendors.
4. Identify privacy safeguards and gaps.
5. Assess vendor processing boundaries.
6. Map findings to Canadian privacy principles.
7. Convert findings into privacy risk statements.
8. Define remediation actions and closure criteria.
9. Link findings, risks, and remediation actions to sanitized evidence references.

## Assessment Inputs

The assessment used sanitized references from a customer-facing reservation and payment-related application, including:

- Data flow documentation.
- Access control analysis.
- Control testing observations.
- Walkthrough notes.
- Sanitized screenshots or evidence descriptions.
- Privacy inventory outputs.
- Framework mapping outputs.
- Risk and remediation artifacts.

No real customer data, credentials, confidential production exports, or proprietary business records are included in the public portfolio package.

## Assessment Lens

The assessment used Canadian privacy principles as an analytical lens, primarily the PIPEDA Fair Information Principles.

The assessment focused on:

- Accountability.
- Identifying purposes.
- Consent considerations.
- Limiting collection.
- Limiting use, disclosure, and retention.
- Accuracy.
- Safeguards.
- Openness.
- Individual access considerations.
- Challenging compliance support.

This assessment does not claim that the application is compliant with PIPEDA or any other privacy law.

## Risk Analysis Approach

Risks were assessed using directional scoring:

```text
Inherent Risk Score = Impact x Likelihood
```

Risk ratings are directional:

| Score Range | Rating |
|---|---|
| 1 to 7 | Low |
| 8 to 15 | Medium |
| 16 and above | High |

This is not a formal quantitative model. It is used to support consistent portfolio-level risk prioritization.

## Evidence Approach

Evidence is referenced through an Evidence Index rather than embedded directly in each deliverable.

Each evidence item includes:

- Evidence ID.
- Evidence name.
- Related finding or risk.
- Evidence type.
- Source.
- Description.
- Sanitization applied.
- Date reviewed.
- Reliability.
- Notes.

This improves traceability while avoiding exposure of sensitive material.

## Limitations

This methodology does not include:

- Full source code review.
- Penetration testing.
- Formal legal advice.
- Regulatory audit.
- Vendor audit.
- Compliance certification.
- Full privacy program implementation.
- Full data subject request program implementation.
- Full breach response program implementation.

## Disclaimer

This methodology note is part of a public-safe portfolio project. It is not a formal legal opinion, official Privacy Impact Assessment methodology, regulatory audit methodology, certification, or compliance attestation.
