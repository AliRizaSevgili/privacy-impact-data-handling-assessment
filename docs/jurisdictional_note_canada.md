# Jurisdictional Note: Canada

## Purpose

This document explains the Canadian privacy context used in the Privacy Impact & Data Handling Risk Assessment: Customer-Facing Reservation Platform project.

The project uses Canadian privacy principles as an assessment lens. It does not provide legal advice and does not conclude compliance with any federal or provincial privacy law.

## Primary Assessment Lens

The primary assessment lens is the PIPEDA Fair Information Principles.

These principles are used to structure assessment questions around:

- Accountability.
- Identifying purposes.
- Consent.
- Limiting collection.
- Limiting use, disclosure, and retention.
- Accuracy.
- Safeguards.
- Openness.
- Individual access.
- Challenging compliance.

## Why PIPEDA Principles Were Used

The assessed system is a customer-facing reservation and payment-related application. It involves collection, use, storage, access, third-party processing, retention, and deletion of customer personal information.

The PIPEDA Fair Information Principles provide a practical structure for analyzing those privacy themes in a Canadian private-sector context.

## Provincial Privacy Laws

This project does not perform a detailed provincial law compliance analysis.

The following areas are intentionally out of scope unless separately assessed:

- Quebec Law 25 compliance assessment.
- Ontario public-sector FIPPA assessment.
- Ontario municipal MFIPPA assessment.
- Ontario PHIPA assessment.
- Alberta PIPA assessment.
- British Columbia PIPA assessment.
- Public-sector Privacy Impact Assessment submission.

## Quebec Note

Quebec privacy law may impose specific requirements for organizations operating in Quebec or processing personal information in a Quebec context.

This project does not claim Quebec Law 25 compliance. If the assessed organization operated in Quebec, a separate Quebec-specific review would be required.

## Health Information Note

This project does not assess personal health information and does not perform a PHIPA analysis.

## Payment Processing Note

Stripe hosted checkout is treated as a third-party payment processing dependency. This project does not audit Stripe, review Stripe SOC reports, certify PCI DSS compliance, or provide a vendor assurance opinion.

## Data Subject Rights Note

The project includes a data subject request intake template for demonstration purposes. It does not claim that a full operational request-handling program has been implemented.

## Breach Readiness Note

The project includes a breach triage record template for demonstration purposes. It does not claim that a full breach response program has been implemented.

## Disclaimer

This jurisdictional note is for portfolio and educational purposes. It is not legal advice, a regulatory opinion, or a compliance certification.
